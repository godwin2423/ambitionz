If Clause 

A clause contains subject and verb. Clauses can be mainly classified in to 2:

Main (Principal) (പൂർണം) - A full stop can be put after a clause.
Subordinate clause - A full stop cannot be put after a clause. The word used to make a clause subordinate is used only at the beginning of the sentence.

Eg: I saw him when I reached home. Which is the subordinate clause?

I saw her/ I reached home/ I saw him when/ None of these

If clause/ subordinate clause Main clause/ principal clause
Simple present tense (Subject + v1) Subject + will/shall/can/may + base verb

Eg: If he works hard, he will pass.
Note: when the subject is singular the verb comes with ‘s’
Eg: He writes, You write, They write, I write

If clause/ subordinate clause Main clause/ principal clause
Simple past tense (Subject + v2) Subject + would/could/should/might + base verb

Eg: If he worked hard he would pass.

If clause/ subordinate clause Main clause/ principal clause
Past perfect (subject + had + v3) Subject + would/should/could/might + have + v3

Eg: If he had worked hard he would have passed.

When a noun comes after have or has it is simple present tense. When a noun comes after had it is simple past tense. ക്കുക -> verb   eg: കുടിക്കുക
For imaginary things the verb in the if clause should be plural in past tense.

Active voice – Passive voice

A sentence beginning with subject of the statement is called active voice.

A sentence beginning with object of the statement is called passive voice.

Object + is/are/am/was/were/be/being/been + v3 + by + subject -> Passive voice

Object

The verb which can answer to the questions what/whom/ to whom (ആരെ / എന്ത് / ആർക്ക്) are transitive verbs, others are intransitive verbs. Answer to these questions are the object of the statement. Answer to the first 2 questions is the direct object and answer to ‘to whom’ is the indirect object.
