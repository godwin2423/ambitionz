If Clause 

A clause contains subject and verb. Clauses can be mainly classified in to 2:

Main (Principal) (പൂർണം) - A full stop can be put after a clause.
Subordinate clause - A full stop cannot be put after a clause. The word used to make a clause subordinate is used only at the beginning of the sentence.

Eg: I saw him when I reached home. Which is the subordinate clause?

I saw her/ I reached home/ I saw him when/ None of these

If clause/ subordinate clause Main clause/ principal clause
Simple present tense (Subject + v1) Subject + will/shall/can/may + base verb

Eg: If he works hard, he will pass.
Note: when the subject is singular the verb comes with ‘s’
Eg: He writes, You write, They write, I write

If clause/ subordinate clause Main clause/ principal clause
Simple past tense (Subject + v2) Subject + would/could/should/might + base verb

Eg: If he worked hard he would pass.

If clause/ subordinate clause Main clause/ principal clause
Past perfect (subject + had + v3) Subject + would/should/could/might + have + v3

Eg: If he had worked hard he would have passed.

When a noun comes after have or has it is simple present tense. When a noun comes after had it is simple past tense. ക്കുക -> verb   eg: കുടിക്കുക
For imaginary things the verb in the if clause should be plural in past tense.

1. He would have come if I ______ him.
Invite/ Invited/ had invited/ invites

2. If I got a job I _____ my family.
Would have helped/ Will help/ Would help/ Help

3. If I had money I ____ a watch.
Will buy/ Would buy/ Would have bought

4. If I ____ you I would not buy the bike.
Am/ Is/ Were/Have been

5. If I have time I ____ her.
Will follow/ Would follow/ Would have followed/ Followed

6. The car would have knocked her down if the driver ____ the brake.
Applies/Had applied/ Applied/ Had not applied

7. Had you encouraged I _____ the match.
Will win/ Would have won/ Would win/ None of these

Note: If you had encouraged

Active voice – Passive voice

A sentence beginning with subject of the statement is called active voice. A sentence beginning with object of the statement is called passive voice.
Object + is/are/am/was/were/be/being/been + v3 + by + subject -> Passive voice

Object :- The verb which can answer to the questions what/whom/ to whom (ആരെ / എന്ത് / ആർക്ക്) are transitive verbs, others are intransitive verbs. Answer to these questions are the object of the statement. Answer to the first 2 questions is the direct object and answer to ‘to whom’ is the indirect object.

He has given some money to a beggar. What is the indirect object (IO) and direct object (DO) in the sentence?
He - money/ Money – beggar/ He – beggar/ None of these
Ans. Beggar - money

Write a letter -> I write what/whom/ to whom -> letter (object)

When the subject is singular it comes with ‘s’. Plural -> without ‘s’.
