Direct – Indirect  / Reported Speech

Sentences can be classified in to 4.
I. Declarative/ Assertive/ Statement/ prasthavana : - A sentence beginning with a subject and ending with a full stop.

Indirect form:-

1) Reporting verb – It is seen outside the inverted commas.
Said -> Said
Said to -> told
Says -> says
Says to -> tells
Say to -> tell

2) Avoid the inverted commas using ‘that’.

3) First person & second person are changed to third person.
I -> Njan.
We -> Ningal.
We -> Eniku.
My -> Ente.
Us -> Namukku.
Our -> Nammude.
You -> Nee, Ningal, Ninne, Ninaku.
Your -> Ninte, Ningalude.
Yours -> Nintethu, Ningaludethu.
He -> Avan.
Him -> Avanu, Avane.
His -> Avante.
She -> Aval.
Her -> Avalude, Avale, Avalku.
Hers -> Avaludethu.
They -> Avar.
Their -> Avarude.
Them -> Avare, Avarku.

4) v1 -> v2
v2 -> had v3

5) When there is helping verb in the statement, the change is occurred to it.
6) I, we – shall is used. For others will is used.
7) When the reporting verb is in the present tense, tense of the verb in the statement should not change.

8) In universal truth, the tense of the verb in the statement should not change.
this -> that
these -> those
now -> then
here -> there
ago -> before
thus -> so
must -> had to
come -> go
today -> that day
yesterday -> the day before
tomorrow -> the next day/ the following day

“I like chocolates”, he said – He said that he liked chocolates.
“You must come here tomorrow”, he said  - He said that I had to go there the next day.
“I love you”, he says – He says that he loves her.
“The sun rises in the east”, the teacher taught – The teacher taught that the sun rises in the east.

II. Imperative sentence/ order or request.
A sentence beginning with a main verb. Eg: Open the door.

1) Reporting verb – Ordered, requested, asked.
2) Add – ‘to’.
3) Tense of the verb in the statement should not change.

“Open the door”, he said – He ordered to open the door.
“Do not open the door”, he said -  He ordered not to open the door.

III. Exclamatory sentence.
A sentence ending with exclamation mark.

Exclamatory words:-
What – used before plural nouns.
What a – used before singular nouns.
How – the word is used to very in the statement.

Statement means Subject + verb.
In an exclamatory sentence, usually the subject and verb come at the end.

What a beautiful flower it is! – Change in to a statement.
It is a beautiful flower.
How horrible! – Change in to a statement.
It is very horrible.

Indirect speech of exclamatory sentence.
1) Reporting verb should be exclaimed.
20 Add – ‘that’.

“What beautiful flower they are!”, she said – She exclaimed that the flowers were beautiful.
“Alaas! A snake”, she said – She exclaimed with fear that there was a snake.
“Hurrah! I have won the game” he said – He exclaimed with joy that he had won the game.

IV. Interrogative sentence.
A sentence ending with a question mark.

Direct question Indirect question
Question word + be (is/are/ was/were) + subject + ? Question word + subject + be + full stop
(Question word) + helping verb + subject + main verb + ? (Question word) + subject + helping verb + main verb + full stop
