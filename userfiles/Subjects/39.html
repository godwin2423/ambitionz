Preamble

Historical background:-
1946 Dec 13 – objective resolution by Nehru.
1947 Jan 22 – acceptance of preamble. Model of preamble from USA. Architect of preamble – Nehru.

Important ingredients:-

  1. Sources of authority – we the people of India.
  2. Nature of Indian constitution – Sovereign Socialist Secular Democratic Republic.
  3. Objective of Indian constitution – Justice, Liberty, Equality and Fraternity.
  4. Date of adoption of Indian constitution – 1949 Nov 26.

People words:-

  1. Identity card – NA. Palkiwala – bitterly flying with ID card.
  2. Political horoscope – KM. Munshi – musnhi selling fish.
  3. Keynote – Earnest Baker – bakery opening with key and seeing note.
  4. Soul and key – Nehru – Nehru correcting his damaged shoe with key.
  5. Key, Jewel, Yard stick, Heart and soul – Dhakur Das Bhargava – Bharva opening shop with key, seeing jewel, using stick to take the jewel, blue light from heart.

Important terms:-

  1. Sovereign – independent to conduct India’s affairs.
  2. Socialist – added by 42nd amendment – it means democratic socialism. Leaning towards Gandhian socialism.
  3. Secular – added by 42nd amendment.
  4. Democracy – indirect democracy as well as representative parliament democracy.
  5. Republic – having an elected head.
  6. Justice – taken from Russian revolution (1917).
  7. Liberty, Equality, Fraternity – taken from French revolution (1789) – The term integrity added by 42nd amendment of 1976.

Controversy of preamble:-

  1. Berubasi union case – 1960 – preamble is not part of Indian constitution.
  2. Kesavananda Bharathi case – 1973 – preamble is part of Indian constitution.

Citizenship:-

Part 2 – Article 5 to 11.

  1. Art 5 – this article is for those who are living in India before 1950 Jan 26. Need five years as ordinarily resident.
  2. Art 6 – this article is for those who migrated from Pakistan. Before 1948 Jul 19 no registration required. After 1948 Jul 19, 6 months stay and registration required to get citizenship.
  3. Art 7 – this article is for those who migrated to Pakistan and later return to India after 1947 Mar 1.  Need 6 months stay and registration.
  4. Art 8 – Person of Indian origin (4th generation)
  5. Art 9 – single citizenship and cancellation of citizenship. If any person acquires citizenship of another country, his Indian citizenship will be cancelled.
  6. Art 10 – citizenship will be according to law provisions of Parliament.
  7. Art 11 – parliament can create law for Indian citizenship.
