1.Positive adjective – An adjective not ending with er/ est or not starting with more or must.
No other + common noun + complement of the common noun + verb + so + positive adjective + as + subject.
a) Hari is the cleverest boy in the class – change in to positive.
No other boy in the class is so clever as Hari.
b) The Nile is the longest river in the world – change in to positive.
No other river in the world is so long as the Nile.

When ‘one of the’ come in superlative or ‘most other’ in comparative, ‘very few’ instead of ‘no other’ is used in positive.
a) The Nile is one of the longest rivers in the world.
Very few rivers in the world are so long as the Nile.

2.Comparitive adjective – Adjective ending with ‘er’ or starting with ‘more’ including less and worse.
Subject + verb + comparative adjective + than + any other + common noun + complement.
a) Hari is the cleverest boy in the class.
Hari is cleverer than any other boy in the class.
b) The Nile is the longest river in the world.
The Nile is longer than any other river in the world.

When ‘one of the’ come in superlative or ‘very few’ in positive ‘most other’ instead of ‘any other’ is used in comparative.
a) Bangalore is one of the most beautiful cities in India.
Bangalore is more beautiful than most other cities in India.

3.Superlative adjective – Adjectives ending with ‘est’ or staring with ‘the most’.
Subject + verb + the + superlative adjective + common noun + complement.
a) No other boy in the class is so handsome as Sudheesh – change in to superlative.
Sudheesh is the most handsome boy in the class.

When ‘very few’ comes in positive or ‘most other’ in comparative -
Subject + verb + one of the + plural common noun + complement.
a) Very few animals in the world are so strong as the elephant.
Elephant is one of the strongest animals in the world.
