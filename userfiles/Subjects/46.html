Articles

‘The’ is used before the following words.
Name of metals, name of meals – particularly say about these, ‘The’ is used.
Price of gold is increasing.
The gold I gave him on that day was very precious.

‘The’ is not used before the names of languages. When ‘The’ is used it becomes people of that country. When the word ‘language’ comes after the name of a language, ‘The’ is used.
Eg: The English language.

The French speak the French.
French speak the French.
The French speak French language.
The French speak French. – correct anaswer.

Names of games:- ‘The’ is not used with the names of games.
Eg: He usually  plays cricket.

Indefinite article a/an
The word beginning with ‘aeiou’ and silent ‘h’ – ‘an’ is used.
hour, honour, heir/heiress – h is silent.
I am a member of parliament.
I am an MP – ‘an’ is used if ‘aeiou’ sound comes when we split the first letter of the abbreviation.
I have an MA.
I have a BA.

‘a’ is used with all the words – when the first letter sounds like ‘u’.
A university, a unicorn, a unique.

Year – ‘a’ is used.
one (wone) – a is used.
A one rupee note.

Degrees of comparison

Elder/ older:- elder/ eldest is used in the case of one family, for others older/ oldest is used.
He is my eldest son.

Comparative adjectives:- an adjective ending with ‘er’ or starting with ‘more’ (including less and worse) are the comparison of two things. But superlative adjectives are (ending with ‘est’ or starts with ‘most’) the comparison of more than 2 things.
Usually ‘the’ is not used before comparative adjectives except ‘the more the more’.
Raju is taller than any other boy in the class.
Of the 2 languages I am learning Telungu is easier.
Of the 2 sons I have Raju is the elder.
Of the 2 boys he is the cleverer.

If mentioned about 2 items – ‘the’ is used. Before superlative adjectives ‘the’ must be used.
The tallest boy. The biggest one.

Usually ‘than’ is used after comparative adjectives but for a few words ‘to’ is used.
Senior to, junior to, superior to, inferior to, prior to, prefer to, elder to.

than + I/we/you/he/she/they…
to + me/us/you/him/her/them…

He is senior to me.
Of my 2 sisters she is ___ (eldest/the oldest/ the elder/ none of these (ans.)) – ans –elder.
Of my 3 friends he is _____ (the cleverer/ cleverer/ cleverest/ none of these (ans.)) – ans – the cleverest.
He is/ elder than/I./ no error (ans. b – correct – older than).
Higher we climb/ the cooler/ we feel./ no error (Ans. d – correct – the higher we climb – the more the more format).
The more we read/ more knowledge/ we get/ no error (ans. b – correct – the more knowledge.)

Adjectives can be classified in to 3:- positive, comparative and superlative.

Worse than – correct. Less than – correct. Lesser than –wrong. Worser than – wrong.
