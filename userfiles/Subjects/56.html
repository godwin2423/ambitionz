Electricity
1.    Unit of electric charge – coulomb.
2.    The value of 1 coulomb – 6.25 x 10 ^18 electrons.
3.    Value of 1 electron – 1.6 x 10 ^-19 coulomb.
4.    The instrument used for showing electric charge – Capacitor/ Condenser.
5.    Instrument used for finding electric charge – Electroscope.

Electric current
6.    Unit of electric current – ampere or coulomb/sec.
7.    The instrument used for finding electric current – ammeter.
8.    Instrument used for finding direction of current – Galvanometer.
9.    Instrument used for changing direction of current – Commutator.
10.    First electricity observed substance – amber.

AC
11.    The voltage(direction) changing current is known as – AC.
12.    The instrument used for increasing AC voltage – Step up transformer.
13.    Instrument used for decreasing AC voltage – step down transformer.
14.    Working principle of transformer – Electro magnetic induction.
15.    Production of magnetic field in a c conductor while flowing current is known as electro magnetism.

16.    Production of electric field in a conductor by the electro magnetism is known as electro magnetic induction.
17.    Father of electro magnetism – Hans Orested(1820)
18.    Father of electromagnetic induction, father of electricity, electric field, electrolysis, dynamo – Michael Farade.
19.    Production of electricity from a chemical solution is known as electrolysis.
20.    The chemical solution that is used in electrolysis is known as electrolyte.

21.    Common examples of electrolyte – Hydrochloric acid, potassium chloride, Sodium chloride.
22.    The region around electric charge or electric current is electric field.
23.    Energy convention in dynamo – mechanical energy to electrical energy.
24.    The instrument used for converting AC to DC – Rectifier (rectification)
25.    Instrument used for converting DC to AC – Oscillator

DC
26.    The single voltage or direction current is known as DC.
27.    The instrument used for passing DC and block AC – inductor.

Potential difference/ emf/ electromotive force/ electric potential
28.    Unit of emf – volt.
29.    The instrument used for finding emf – volt meter.
30.    Instrument used for finding emf of different devices at same time – potentio meter.
31.    The law that is related to emf – Ohms law (V = IR at constant temp)

32.    Father of ohms law – George Simon Ohm.
33.    The unit of resistance – ohm.
34.    The unit of specific resistance – Ohm meter.
35.    The instrument used for changing resistance in a circuit – Rheostat.

Cell
36.    The cell that is used for converting chemical energy to electric energy is known as Primary cell.
37.    Example of primary cell – Daniel cell, Dry cell, voltaic cell.
38.    The cell that is used for converting electric energy to chemical energy – secondary cell.
39.    Example of secondary cell  - Edison-Alkali cell, Lead acid cell.
40.    The cell that is used in mobile devices – Lithium-ion cell.

41.    Emf of Lithium-ion cell – 3.6 volt.
42.    Emf of torch cell or remote cell – 1.5 volt.
43.    The emf of voltaic cell – 1 volt.
44.    The cell that is used in electrolysis process – voltaic cell.
45.    The collection of the cell is known as battery.
46.    Father of cell – Alexander Volta.

Super conductor
47.    The metals passing current at very low temperature is known as Super conductor.
48.    The best super conductor – Ceramics (Silver id the best conductor).
49.    The material production at very low temp is known as Cryogenic technology.
50.    Father of super conductor – Kamerling Onnes.

Joules Law
51.    The heating effect of electricity is known as Joules law – found by Joule Thomson.
52.    Equation of Joules Law – H = I^2 RT, where H is losting current as heat, I is current, R is resistance, T is time.

Home current electricity
53.    The frequency of home current – 50 Hz.
54.    Voltage of home current – 220-230 V.
55.    The unit used in home current electricity – KWh (Kilo watt hour)
56.    The value of 1KWh – 3.6 x 10^6 joule.
57.    Which type electricity is used in home – AC.
58.    The component of fuse – Alloy of lead and tin.

Magnetism
59.    The substances which show weak magnetism when placed in a magnetic field is known as para magnetic substances. (Palladium, sodium, osmium, platinum, oxygen, manganese)
60.    The substances which show repulsion when placed in a magnetic field known as dia magnetism (Hydrogen, water, alcohol, inert gases (the atoms which doesn’t involve any chemical reaction – helium, neon, argon, xenon, krypton, radon), gold, silver, lead, copper, zinc, antimony, bismuth).
61.    The substances which show strong magnetism when placed in a magnetic field is known as ferro magnetism (Eg. iron, cobalt, Nickel, refrigerator magnet)
62.    The temp that is needed to convert a ferro magnetic substance to para magnetic substance – Currie temp.

63.    Currie temp of iron – 770 deg Celsius. 
64.    The material is known as natural magnet – Lode stone (leading stone).
65.    The component of Lode stone – magnate.
66.    The direction showing a magnet when suspended at free – NS.
67.    The region around a magnet is known as magnetic field.

68.    The direction showing a magnetic field or magnetic field lines at outside the magnet – NS.
69.    The direction showing magnetic field at inside the magnet – SN.
70.    Father of ferro magnetism – Louis Neil.
71.    A magnet is made by the help of electricity is known as artificial/ electro magnet.
72.    The ability of the animal to knowing the presence of magnetic field is known as magnetoception.
73.      The unit of magnetic field – tesla.

Sound
74.    The study of sound – acoustics .
75.    The velocity of sound in air – 340 m/s
76.    In which state of mater the velocity of sound is maximum – solid.
77.    In which solid substance the velocity of sound is max – Aluminum (second Nickel, third steel).
78.    In which state of matter the velocity of the sound is minimum – gas.
79.    In which gas the velocity of sound is max – Hydrogen.

80.    In which liquid velocity of sound is max – sea water (second distilled water).
81.    Sound producing organ of human – Larynx.
82.    When temp increases velocity of sound increases.
83.    Sound cannot travel through vacuum.
84.    Speed of the object exceed the velocity of sound – supersonic.
85.    Loud sound that associate with supersonic object – sonic boom.
86.    Large energy that is associated with supersonic object – shock energy.
87.    Speed of supersonic object – mac number (1mac = 340m/s).
