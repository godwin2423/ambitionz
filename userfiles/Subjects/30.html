Science – Space missions
1.    India’s 100th space mission – PSLV C21 – was successfully launched on 9th Sep 2012 at Sriharikotta, Andra.
2.    India’s 101 first space mission – PSLV C20 was successfully launched on 25th Feb 2013 – Sriharikotta. Indo-French satellite Saral.
3.    India’s first navigation satellite – IRNSS 1A (launched by -PSLV C22) was launched on 1st June 2013 Sriharikotta.
4.    India’s second navigation satellite IRNSS 1B (PSLV C24) was successfully launched on 4th Apr 2014.
5.    India’s third navigation satellite IRNSS 1C (PSLV C26) – 16 Oct 2014 – Sriharikotta.

6.    India’s first mars mission – Mangalyan was successfully launched on 5 Nov 2013 – PSLV C25 – SriHarikotta – Project director – Dr. S. Arun. Successfully entered in to mars orbit on -24 Sep 2014.
7.    India’s new space mission – PSLV C23 – was successfully launched on 30 Jun 2014 – SriHarikotta.
8.    India’s first defense satellite – GSAT 7 – was successfully launched on 30 Aug 2013 – French Guyana in South America – launched  by Arena 5.
9.    India’s new telecommunication satellite GSAT 14 launched by GSLVD 5. It used indigenous cryogenic engine – launched on 5 Jan 2014 – Sriharikotta.
10.    India’s latest telecommunication satellite GSAT 16 was successfully launched on 7 Dec 2014 – French Guyana  - Arena 5.

11.    India’s most powerful satellite launch vehicle – GSLV Mark 3 – was successfully launched on 18 Dec 2014 – Sriharikitta – It will be able to put satellite weighing up to 4tons into orbit, almost doubling India’s current capacity, which could grieve the agency of option of manned mission.
